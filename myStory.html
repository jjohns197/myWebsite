My Story



To whom it may concern,

My name is Joshua Johnson, and I am a Computer Science graduate from Towson University. I have had the privilege of navigating a challenging, yet rewarding journey to complete my education, and I am eager to apply my skills and experiences to a professional role in the tech industry.

My academic journey began at Vincennes University, but I paused my education when my mother had a stroke in 2009. This pivotal moment led me to focus on helping my family, and I relocated to Annapolis, Maryland, to support my mom during her recovery. During this time, I worked to support myself and my family, all while remaining committed to one goal: finishing my education.

After enrolling at Anne Arundel Community College (AACC), I juggled full-time work and studies, determined to pursue a degree that would combine my passion for computers, electronics, and problem-solving. Over time, I took on various jobs, including at Capital One and Best Buy, before discovering Uber. Uber provided the flexibility I needed to focus on school, and eventually, I made the decision to work full-time with Uber while continuing my studies.

In the summer of 2024, I graduated from Towson University with a Bachelor's in Computer Science. This achievement represents my perseverance and passion for learning. Throughout my journey, I have developed strong technical skills in areas such as front-end development, SQL, database management, and software engineering. My passion for technology, paired with my diverse work experience, has equipped me with a unique perspective on problem-solving and teamwork.

I am confident that my determination, technical skills, and life experiences will allow me to contribute effectively to any team I join. I am excited about the opportunity to continue learning and growing in a professional setting, and I am open to new challenges that will allow me to apply my knowledge in meaningful ways.

Thank you for your time and consideration. I look forward to the possibility of working together.



Sincerely,
Joshua Johnson
